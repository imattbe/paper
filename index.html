Chapter 1: The Origins

Paper originates from the word papyrus, from the papyrus plant, native to Africa. The plant was used in Ancient Eypyt for making things such as baskets, sandals, blankets, medicine, and of course as a paper precursor. As a metrial it is similar to thick paper and was in use from as far back as the First Dynasty between c. 3100 BC to c. 2900 BC.

Chapter 2: China

The story of paper began in Egypt, with writing on papyrus, a material similar to thick paper from as far back as the First Dynasty between c. 3100 BC to c. 2900 BC. However, true papermaking began around 3000 years later in China during the Eastern Han (東漢), also known as the Later Han, which began when Liu Xiu became Emperor of Guangwi of Han.

The creation of paper is attributed to Cai Lun (蔡伦), a chinese court official
